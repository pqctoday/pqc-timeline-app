# Data & Asset Sensitivity — In Simple Terms

## What This Is About

Not all information is equally important. Your grocery list and your Social Security number both live on your phone, but losing one is an inconvenience and losing the other is a crisis. Data sensitivity is about figuring out which information needs the strongest protection — and doing that systematically across an entire organization.

Think of it like packing for an emergency evacuation. You cannot take everything, so you need to decide quickly: what is irreplaceable? What would cause the most harm if lost or stolen? The same logic applies to preparing for the quantum computing era. Organizations have thousands of databases, applications, and systems. They cannot upgrade the encryption on all of them at the same time, so they need to figure out which ones to protect first.

This also means understanding the rules. Different types of data have different legal requirements. Medical records have health privacy laws. Financial data has banking regulations. Government information has classification rules. Each set of rules may have its own timeline for when quantum-safe encryption must be in place.

## Why It Matters

If an organization treats all data the same, it will either waste resources over-protecting unimportant information or, worse, leave its most sensitive data vulnerable while working on less critical systems first. The "harvest now, decrypt later" threat makes this especially urgent — attackers collecting encrypted data today will go after the most valuable targets first.

Getting the priority wrong can mean regulatory fines, lawsuits, loss of customer trust, or even national security breaches. Organizations that classify their data properly can focus their time and budget where it matters most.

## The Key Takeaway

The first step in any encryption upgrade is knowing what you have and how much it matters. You cannot protect what you have not classified. Prioritizing by sensitivity ensures the most valuable and most regulated data gets quantum-safe protection first.

## What's Happening

Regulatory bodies worldwide are updating their requirements to include quantum-readiness timelines. Frameworks like NIST's Risk Management Framework and Europe's DORA regulation now factor in the quantum threat. Organizations are running data discovery and classification projects — essentially taking inventory of everything they protect and ranking it by how much damage a breach would cause. This classification work is becoming a prerequisite before any large-scale encryption migration begins.
