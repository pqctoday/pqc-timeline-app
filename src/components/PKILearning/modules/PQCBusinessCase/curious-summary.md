# PQC Business Case — In Simple Terms

## What This Is About

Upgrading security costs money. New software, new hardware, staff training, testing — none of it is free. So before any organization spends that money, leaders need to answer a fair question: Is it worth it?

Building a business case for quantum-safe security is like making the argument for home insurance. Your house might never catch fire, but if it does, the cost of not having insurance is catastrophic. The same logic applies here. The cost of upgrading security now is predictable and manageable. The cost of a quantum-enabled breach — stolen customer data, regulatory fines, lawsuits, lost reputation — could be many times larger and completely unpredictable.

## Why It Matters

Without a clear business case, quantum-safe upgrades get stuck in budget meetings and lose out to projects with more obvious short-term returns. Meanwhile, the risk keeps growing. Attackers are already collecting encrypted data today with plans to crack it later using future quantum computers. Every year of delay means more data is exposed to this "harvest now, decrypt later" threat.

The business case also shapes how the upgrade happens. A well-built case helps leaders decide how much to spend, where to start, and how to phase the investment over time — rather than either ignoring the problem or throwing money at it in a panic.

## The Key Takeaway

Investing in quantum-safe security is not just a technology expense — it is risk reduction with measurable returns. The business case compares the known, manageable cost of upgrading now against the potentially enormous cost of being caught unprepared. Organizations that frame it this way get leadership buy-in faster and make smarter spending decisions.

## What's Happening

Major consulting firms and industry groups have published research quantifying the financial risk of quantum threats. Insurance companies are starting to factor quantum readiness into cyber insurance premiums. Government procurement rules are beginning to require quantum-safe capabilities, meaning companies that invest early gain access to contracts that laggards will lose. Some organizations are already reporting quantum readiness as part of their annual security disclosures, turning it into a competitive differentiator.
