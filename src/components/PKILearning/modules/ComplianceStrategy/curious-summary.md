# Compliance & Regulatory Strategy — In Simple Terms

## What This Is About

When governments decide that something is important enough, they write rules that everyone must follow. Speed limits keep roads safe. Food safety regulations keep groceries safe. Now, governments around the world are writing new rules about quantum-safe security — and organizations need to understand them and follow them.

Think of it like building codes for houses. If the government says your building must withstand a certain level of earthquake, you cannot just ignore the rule and hope for the best. Similarly, new regulations are telling organizations that their digital security must be upgraded to survive the era of quantum computing. These rules come from different places — national governments, industry groups, and international standards bodies — and they each have their own timelines and requirements.

## Why It Matters

Organizations that fall behind on compliance face real consequences. There can be financial penalties, loss of government contracts, or restrictions on doing business in certain countries. In regulated industries like banking, healthcare, and defense, failing to meet security standards can mean losing your license to operate.

But compliance is not just about avoiding punishment. Organizations that get ahead of the rules often gain a competitive advantage. Being able to tell customers "we already meet quantum-safe security standards" builds trust and opens doors, especially when bidding for government or enterprise contracts.

## The Key Takeaway

Quantum-safe compliance is not a single rule from a single place. It is a patchwork of requirements from different governments and industries, each on its own timeline. Organizations need to understand which rules apply to them, when the deadlines hit, and how to build a plan that satisfies all of them without duplicating effort.

## What's Happening

The United States has issued executive orders and memoranda requiring federal agencies to inventory their encryption and transition to quantum-safe standards by specific dates. The European Union is coordinating through ENISA and national agencies like France's ANSSI and Germany's BSI. Financial regulators are updating their security frameworks. Industry standards bodies like ISO, ETSI, and the IETF are publishing quantum-safe specifications. The timeline varies by country and sector, but the direction is universal: quantum-safe compliance is becoming mandatory, not optional.
