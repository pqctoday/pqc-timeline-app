# Migration Program Management — In Simple Terms

## What This Is About

Imagine you live in a town that is switching from well water to a new municipal water system. You cannot just flip a switch one day. You need to map every pipe, plan the connections, test the new system, run both systems side by side for a while, and gradually cut over — all without anyone losing water service. That is essentially what migrating to quantum-safe security looks like for an organization.

Migration program management is the discipline of planning, coordinating, and executing this massive changeover. It involves figuring out what needs to change, in what order, with what resources, and on what timeline. It is project management applied to one of the biggest technology transitions in a generation.

## Why It Matters

A poorly managed migration can be worse than no migration at all. If you upgrade some systems but not others, you create gaps that attackers can exploit. If you rush the transition, you risk breaking critical services. If you go too slowly, your data is exposed for longer than necessary.

The stakes are high because encryption touches nearly everything in a modern organization — email, websites, databases, payment systems, remote access, cloud storage, and more. Each of these systems needs to be identified, evaluated, and upgraded in the right order. Missing even one critical system could leave a door wide open.

## The Key Takeaway

Migrating to quantum-safe security is not a single project — it is a program made up of many coordinated projects, each with its own timeline, dependencies, and risks. Success requires a clear inventory of what needs to change, a prioritized roadmap, and a phased approach that keeps systems running safely throughout the transition.

## What's Happening

Governments have set specific deadlines for quantum-safe migration. The United States has directed federal agencies to complete cryptographic inventories and begin transitioning critical systems. The European Union and countries like France, Germany, and the United Kingdom have published migration guidance and timelines. Large enterprises and financial institutions are standing up dedicated migration programs. Industry frameworks now include step-by-step migration playbooks. The common advice across all of these efforts is the same: start with an inventory, prioritize by risk, and begin the transition now — do not wait for a crisis.
